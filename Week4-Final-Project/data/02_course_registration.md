# IIT Bombay Academic Assistant — Knowledge Base
## Document: Course Registration Process

### 1. Pre-Registration

Before each semester begins, students go through a **pre-registration**
window on the Academic Section's ASC (Academic Section) portal. During
pre-registration:
- Students indicate their intended course list (core courses are
  auto-populated based on the branch curriculum; electives are chosen
  manually).
- Elective seats are allocated using a priority/lottery system that
  typically weighs CPI and seniority (later-year students and higher-CPI
  students often get priority for oversubscribed electives), though the
  exact allocation algorithm is set by the Academic Office each semester.
- Pre-registration data is used by departments to plan sections, TAs, and
  classroom allocation, so it is not merely indicative — it strongly
  influences final seat availability.

### 2. Registration (Start of Semester)

At the start of the semester, students confirm/finalize their course list
during the official **registration** period, usually in the first week of
classes. Registration involves:
1. Logging into the ASC portal.
2. Confirming pre-registered courses or making changes if seats remain.
3. Paying/confirming semester fees (registration is typically blocked if
   fees are unpaid or holds exist, e.g., library dues, hostel dues, or
   pending disciplinary matters).

### 3. Add/Drop Period

Following registration, there is a short **Add/Drop window**, generally the
first two weeks of the semester, during which students may:
- **Add** a course, subject to seat availability and instructor consent for
  some courses.
- **Drop** a course entirely, with no entry appearing on the transcript.

After the Add/Drop deadline but before the **Withdrawal deadline** (usually
around mid-semester, close to the mid-sem exam period), students may
withdraw from a course, which results in a "W" grade on the transcript
instead of a numeric grade — this does not affect SPI/CPI but is a
permanent record.

### 4. Minimum and Maximum Credit Load

- Students must register for a minimum number of credits each semester to
  be considered "full-time" (this floor is set by the Academic Office and
  varies slightly by year of study).
- A maximum credit ceiling also applies per semester to prevent overload;
  students with strong CPI may petition their department's UGC (Under
  Graduate Committee) / faculty advisor for an overload exception.

### 5. Faculty Advisor Role

Each student is assigned a **Faculty Advisor (FA)** at the start of their
program. The FA:
- Reviews and approves the student's course plan, especially electives,
  overloads, and audits.
- Is the first point of contact for academic difficulties, probation
  counselling, or requests to drop below the minimum credit load for
  medical/personal reasons.

### 6. Common Registration Issues

- **Clashing timetables**: the portal blocks registration for two courses
  with overlapping slots unless one is an audit/self-study arrangement
  approved by the instructor.
- **Prerequisite not met**: some courses enforce prerequisite checks; a
  student without the prerequisite must get explicit instructor approval
  entered into the system to register.
- **Backlog courses**: students with an "FR" or unresolved "I" grade in a
  core course are usually auto-flagged and must prioritize the backlog
  course, sometimes with limited elective flexibility until it is cleared.
