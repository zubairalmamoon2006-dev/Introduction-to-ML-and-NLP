# IIT Bombay Academic Assistant — Knowledge Base
## Document: Branch Change, Minors, and Honours

### 1. Branch Change (for eligible UG students)

- Branch change is generally open to **first-year students** after their
  second semester results are declared, subject to a minimum CPI cutoff
  that varies by target department and is published each year by the
  Academic Office.
- Seats available for branch change into a given department depend on that
  department's sanctioned intake versus students already admitted directly
  into it; branch change is therefore competitive and CPI-ranked among
  applicants for the same target branch.
- Once a branch change is confirmed, the student's curriculum requirements
  switch to the new branch's curriculum, and prior credits are mapped
  (core courses already completed that overlap with the new branch's
  requirements are typically credited; non-overlapping courses may count as
  free electives).
- Branch change is normally a **one-time, one-directional** process; a
  student generally cannot request a second branch change later.

### 2. Minors

- A **Minor** is a structured set of additional credits (a defined list of
  courses, usually 4–6 courses with lab/project components as applicable)
  in a discipline outside the student's primary branch.
- Students typically apply/register for a minor through the respective
  department after completing a minimum number of core semesters, subject
  to prerequisite courses and, in some departments, a minimum CPI or
  interview/selection process.
- Minor credits are recorded separately on the transcript and generally do
  not count toward the core B.Tech. credit requirement, though they may
  count toward the overall credit load for the semester (subject to the
  max credit ceiling).

### 3. Honours

- **Honours** is typically awarded within the student's own department for
  completing additional advanced coursework/research credits beyond the
  core B.Tech. requirement, often including a research or project
  component supervised by a faculty member.
- Eligibility usually requires maintaining a minimum CPI (department
  specific) and completing the honours-designated credits without a
  backlog in the affected semester.
- A student who fails to complete all honours requirements by graduation
  may still graduate with the base B.Tech. degree without the "Honours"
  designation, provided core requirements are otherwise met.

### 4. Double Minor / Combined Degrees

- Some departments allow **double minors** if credit and scheduling
  constraints permit, though total credit ceilings per semester and
  overall program duration limits still apply.
- Students interested in Dual Degree conversion (B.Tech. + M.Tech.) follow
  a separate, department-specific application and CPI-cutoff process, and
  should consult their department's UGC or PG office notice for exact
  eligibility windows, since these differ meaningfully from Minor/Honours
  criteria.

### 5. Where Rules Are Finalized

Exact CPI cutoffs, seat quotas, and course lists for Branch Change, Minors,
and Honours are published and updated by the Academic Programmes Office
and respective departments each year, and the version in force at the time
of a student's application is the authoritative one.
