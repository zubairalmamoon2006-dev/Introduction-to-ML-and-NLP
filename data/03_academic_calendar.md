# IIT Bombay Academic Assistant — Knowledge Base
## Document: Academic Calendar Structure

### 1. Two-Semester System

IIT Bombay follows a two-semester academic year:
- **Autumn Semester**: typically starts late July, runs through
  November/December.
- **Spring Semester**: typically starts early January, runs through
  April/May.
- An optional **Summer Term** (May–July) is offered for backlog clearance,
  select credit courses, and internships/research (e.g., Summer of Science,
  Summer Internships), but is not a compulsory teaching term for most
  students.

### 2. Typical Semester Structure (approximate week-by-week)

| Phase                          | Approx. Timing (within semester) |
|--------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Registration                   | Week 0 (before/at semester start) |
| Add/Drop window                 | Weeks 1–2                         |
| Regular teaching                | Weeks 1–7                         |
| Mid-Semester Examinations       | Around Week 8–9                   |
| Course Withdrawal deadline      | Shortly after mid-sems             |
| Regular teaching resumes        | Weeks 9–14                         |
| Study/Reading period            | 2–3 days before end-sems           |
| End-Semester Examinations       | Final 1–2 weeks of semester        |
| Result declaration              | 2–4 weeks after End-Sems           |

Exact dates are published by the Academic Office in the official Academic
Calendar released before each semester and can shift due to institute
holidays, festivals, or extraordinary circumstances (e.g., extended breaks,
health emergencies).

### 3. Key Recurring Dates/Events

- **Convocation**: usually held once a year (typically around August).
- **Founder's Day / Institute events**: interspersed through the year and
  do not usually affect the academic calendar directly but may affect
  extracurricular scheduling.
- **Branch Change window**: typically opens after Semester 2 results are
  declared, for eligible first-year students (see Branch Change document).
- **SoP (Summer of Projects) / Summer of Science**: nomination and
  execution windows generally fall between the Spring semester end and the
  Autumn semester start.

### 4. Holidays and Breaks

- A short **Diwali break** typically falls within the Autumn semester
  teaching period.
- A longer **winter break** separates the Autumn and Spring semesters
  (roughly mid-December to early January).
- Spring semester typically has fewer extended breaks before End-Sems,
  aside from institute-designated holidays.

### 5. Where to Find the Authoritative Calendar

The binding, semester-specific Academic Calendar (with exact dates for
registration, add/drop, mid-sems, end-sems, and result declaration) is
published on the **Academic Programmes / Academic Office section of the
IIT Bombay website** at the start of each semester and supersedes any
general description above.
