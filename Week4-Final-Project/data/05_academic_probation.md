# IIT Bombay Academic Assistant — Knowledge Base
## Document: Academic Probation & Performance Policy

### 1. Academic Warning and Probation

Students whose SPI or CPI falls below department/program-specific
thresholds in a semester are typically flagged by the Academic Section:

- **Academic Warning**: the first-level flag, usually triggered when SPI
  in a single semester drops below a set cutoff (commonly cited informally
  around 5.0–5.5, though the exact figure is set and revised by the
  Institute Academic Committee/Senate and may differ across years and
  programs).
- **Academic Probation**: a more serious status triggered by a low CPI
  and/or consecutive semesters of poor SPI. Students on probation are
  typically:
  - Required to meet with their Faculty Advisor and/or the Dean of
    Student Affairs / Academic Affairs at defined checkpoints.
  - Subject to a reduced maximum credit load to allow focus on backlog
    and core courses.
  - Barred from taking on certain extracurricular/leadership
    commitments in council or hostel bodies until the status is
    resolved, depending on the specific council's own bye-laws.

### 2. Backlog Courses

- A **backlog** is a compulsory (core) course not yet passed with at least
  the minimum passing grade (DD).
- Backlog courses generally must be cleared before certain milestones
  (e.g., before being allowed to register for advanced electives, or before
  minor/honours credits can be counted), and repeated backlog failures can
  extend the overall degree duration.
- Students may be required to repeat a backlog course when next offered,
  register for it as a "repeat" entry (which usually replaces the earlier
  FR/FF grade in CPI calculation, though transcript history is retained),
  or in some cases take an equivalent course with department approval.

### 3. Maximum Duration of the Programme

Every degree program (e.g., 4-year B.Tech., 5-year Dual Degree) has a
maximum permissible duration (commonly up to twice the nominal program
length) within which a student must complete all degree requirements.
Students approaching this limit due to repeated backlogs or breaks are
counselled proactively by their department and the Dean of Academic
Affairs' office, and in persistent cases may face **discontinuation** from
the program per Institute Ordinances.

### 4. Readmission and Break of Study

- Students who are discontinued or who take an approved **break of study**
  (e.g., for medical reasons, or to pursue a specific opportunity with
  Institute approval) may apply for **readmission**, generally reviewed by
  a committee that considers the student's prior academic record and
  reason for the break.
- Readmission is not automatic and is subject to seat availability and
  Senate-level policy at the time of the application.

### 5. Support Mechanisms

Students facing academic difficulty are encouraged to use:
- **Faculty Advisor consultations** (first point of contact).
- **Department-level mentoring/TA support** for specific courses.
- **Counselling and Psychological Wellness services** if the difficulty
  stems from personal, mental health, or adjustment challenges rather than
  purely academic ones.
