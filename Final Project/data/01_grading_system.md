# IIT Bombay Academic Assistant — Knowledge Base
## Document: Grading System and Performance Indices

### 1. Letter Grades and Grade Points

IIT Bombay uses a 10-point relative/absolute grading system. Each course is
assigned one of the following letter grades at the end of a semester:

| Grade | Grade Point | Description                     |
|-------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| AA    | 10          | Excellent                       |
| AB    | 9           | Very Good                       |
| BB    | 8           | Good                             |
| BC    | 7           | Above Average                    |
| CC    | 6           | Average                          |
| CD    | 5           | Below Average                    |
| DD    | 4           | Marginal (minimum passing grade) |
| FR    | 0           | Fail — required to Repeat        |
| FF    | 0           | Fail                              |
| AU    | -           | Audit (no grade point, not counted in SPI/CPI) |
| I     | -           | Incomplete (temporary, must be resolved next semester) |
| S/X   | -           | Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory (used for some project & seminar courses) |
| W     | -           | Withdrawn (course dropped after the add/drop deadline but before the withdrawal deadline) |

DD is the minimum grade point (4) at which a course is considered "passed."
A grade of FR normally means the student must repeat the course the next
time it is offered, while FF is a fail that may or may not require a repeat
depending on department rules.

### 2. SPI and CPI

- **SPI (Semester Performance Index)**: the credit-weighted average of grade
  points earned in a single semester.
  `SPI = Σ(credits_i × grade_point_i) / Σ(credits_i)`
  summed over all courses registered in that semester (excluding AU, W, and
  courses with "I" grade until resolved).

- **CPI (Cumulative Performance Index)**: the credit-weighted average of
  grade points across *all* semesters completed so far, computed the same
  way but summed over the full academic history.

Both SPI and CPI are reported on a 0–10 scale. A CPI of 5.5 or higher is
generally required to remain in "good academic standing"; falling below
department- and program-specific thresholds can trigger academic probation
(see the Academic Probation & Performance document).

### 3. Grade Grievance / Revaluation

Students who wish to contest a grade may:
1. Approach the course instructor within a stipulated window (typically
   within 1–2 weeks of grade publication) to discuss the evaluation.
2. If unresolved, submit a formal grievance to the Academic Programmes
   office / Dean of Academic Affairs, who may constitute a review committee.
3. Grade changes after the grievance window closes are rare and require
   Senate-level approval in most departments.

### 4. Credit System

Each course carries a fixed number of credits (typically 6, 8, or 9 for a
theory/lab combination course, and lower for seminar/audit courses). A
student's total credit requirement for the B.Tech. degree is distributed
across core, departmental elective, humanities/social sciences (HSS), and
minor/honours credits, as laid out in the branch-specific curriculum
structure published by the Academic Office each admission year.
