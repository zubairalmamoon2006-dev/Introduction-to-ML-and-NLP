# IIT Bombay Academic Assistant — Knowledge Base
## Document: Examination Rules and Conduct

### 1. Types of Examinations

- **Quizzes**: short, in-class assessments held periodically through the
  semester (weightage decided by the instructor within institute-approved
  bounds).
- **Mid-Semester Examination (Mid-Sem)**: a formal, centrally-scheduled
  exam around the middle of the semester.
- **End-Semester Examination (End-Sem)**: the major, centrally-scheduled
  exam at the end of the semester, usually carrying the largest weightage.
- Some courses may substitute a portion of exam weightage with projects,
  presentations, or lab evaluations, as specified in the course's approved
  evaluation scheme circulated at the start of the semester.

### 2. Evaluation Scheme Disclosure

Every instructor must circulate the **evaluation scheme** (weightage of
quizzes, mid-sem, end-sem, assignments, attendance if applicable) at the
start of the semester, typically in the first week, and cannot materially
change it afterward without departmental approval and prior notice to
students.

### 3. Examination Conduct Rules

- Students must carry their **ID card** to every examination; entry may be
  refused without it, subject to invigilator discretion and a
  verification process.
- Use of unauthorized material (notes, phones, smartwatches, or
  communication devices) during a closed-book exam is treated as
  **academic malpractice / use of unfair means (UFM)**.
- Only material explicitly permitted in the evaluation scheme (e.g.,
  "open notes," "open book," specific formula sheets) may be used; anything
  beyond that is a violation even if the exam is otherwise "open" in some
  form.
- Impersonation (having someone else appear for an exam) is treated as a
  severe disciplinary offense, handled by the Institute's disciplinary
  bodies rather than the course instructor alone.

### 4. Handling of Unfair Means (UFM) Cases

1. The invigilator reports the incident with evidence (confiscated
   material, written note, etc.) to the Department/Academic Office.
2. A **UFM Committee** (constituted per department or at the institute
   level for serious cases) reviews the evidence and may call the student
   for a hearing.
3. Outcomes can range from a **zero in that component/course**, a
   **grade penalty**, **suspension for a semester**, to **expulsion** for
   repeated or severe offenses — the exact penalty depends on the severity
   and the committee's findings, guided by the Institute's Ordinances on
   student discipline.
4. Decisions can typically be appealed to the Senate Disciplinary
   Committee or equivalent body within a specified window.

### 5. Medical / Emergency Absence from Exams

- A student unable to appear for a Mid-Sem or End-Sem due to documented
  medical or family emergency reasons must inform their instructor and the
  Academic Office as soon as possible, generally submitting a medical
  certificate from an approved (often institute-affiliated) medical
  authority.
- Depending on department policy, this can result in a **make-up
  examination**, an **"I" (Incomplete) grade** to be resolved early next
  semester, or evaluation based on the remaining components at the
  instructor's discretion — the specific route is decided case-by-case by
  the instructor in consultation with the department.

### 6. Grade Publication and Verification

After End-Sem evaluation, grades are uploaded to the ASC portal. Students
should verify their grades and credits registered are correctly reflected;
discrepancies should be reported promptly through the Grade
Grievance/Revaluation process (see Grading System document) since there is
typically a limited correction window each semester.
